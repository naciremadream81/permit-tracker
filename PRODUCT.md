# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Permit expeditors — professionals who manage permit package submissions on behalf of multiple clients (contractors, developers, business owners). They juggle many active permit packages across different jurisdictions and projects simultaneously, often under deadline pressure. Their primary context is a work session: checking status, logging updates, and flagging blockers across a portfolio of open permits.

## Product Purpose

A permit package tracker that gives expeditors a clear, real-time view of every permit's status — from submission through approval. It eliminates the cognitive overhead of tracking across email threads, spreadsheets, and municipal portals. Success means an expeditor can open the app, immediately see what needs attention today, and act on it without digging.

## Brand Personality

Dependable, precise, calm. The app feels like a trusted colleague who always knows exactly where things stand — never anxious, never vague.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards: interchangeable blue/white, the Salesforce aesthetic, sidebar-nav-plus-table-grid boilerplate
- Construction management tools: heavy enterprise energy, Procore / Autodesk aesthetic
- Overly minimal / cold: too much whitespace, no warmth, sterile or featureless
- Government / municipal portals: bureaucratic, dated, dense tables everywhere

## Design Principles

1. **Status at a glance** — every screen should answer "what needs attention?" before the user has to ask
2. **Calm confidence** — the interface never looks overwhelmed, even when managing dozens of active packages
3. **Earned trust** — information density without noise; every piece of data earns its place on screen
4. **One source of truth** — the app is the record, not a mirror of scattered emails; it should feel authoritative
5. **Human but professional** — warmth and approachability without sacrificing the precision expeditors depend on

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Ensure sufficient contrast for status indicators (color is never the only signal for status — always pair with icon or label). Support keyboard navigation for power users who move fast.
